Launching Rockets With LUNAR

LUNAR flies from two sites: Snow Ranch, 30 minutes east of Stockton on California Highway 4, and Brigantino Park in Hollister, California.

(T)ARC teams are welcome to fly at our launches, but must be aware they are sharing the site with other fliers and cannot expect priority.

Launches are open to all LUNAR members, and non-members pay a $20 fee per group.

Snow Ranch

Next Launch: Saturday, January 3rd, 2026

The Snow Ranch launch site is located on California Highway 4 about 30 miles east of Stockton, California.

The Ranch is about an hour drive from Livermore. The field is certified Level 3 with a 15,000 foot ceiling. We are currently allowing up to M motors on that field. You will need to get LUNAR board approval before flying L or M class engines (> 2,560 N).

The launch site is on an operating cattle ranch so normal ranch rules apply.

  • If you open a gate, close it.
  • Don't bother the bulls, cows, and especially the calves.
  • Don't tear up the turf.
  • Stay on the marked roads. In the wet months your car will get stuck in the mud if you stray off the road.
  • There are wild animals in the area, including squirrels, rabbits, rattle snakes, coyotes, eagles, and mountain lions. Keep an eye on your children and don't stick your hands and feet where you cannot see. Especially stay away from the rock fences, the black berry bushes, and the brushy rock formations. They are full of snakes but they won't bother you if you don't bother them.

There are no amenities at the field so bring everything that you will need. We will rent a porta potty for the event.

Note, however, that you can only count on flying motors up through G-class on a drop-in basis (conditions permitting.) Flying H and larger motors requires both HPR Level 1 certification (NAR or TRA) and the presence of a licensed Class III Pyrotechnician to supervise the flight. There are a few licensees in the club, so it is likely (but not guaranteed) that one will be on hand at any given launch.

Brigantino Park

Next Launch: None Scheduled

The Brignatino Park Launch Site is located in Hollister, CA, about an hour south of the Bay Area. Launches are limited to low- and mid-power (A through G motors) with a maximum altitude of 1000 feet.

The launch location on this field is about 200 yards from the parking lot. You will not be able to easily operate out of your car so be prepared to haul your rockets, tables, chairs, etc. to the launch location. A wagon would be very useful.

The park is next to a ridge covered in oak trees, and poison oak vines, so be mindful of launch angles, winds, and recovery gear.

Bathrooms are available by the parking lot.
